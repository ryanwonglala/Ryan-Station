Engineering in Motion

I am currently pursuing my Master's degree in Robotics&Automation. I believe that great automation comes from the perfect synchronization of hardware design and software control. This website is my digital lab bench, where I document my learning process, course projects, and my exploration into the future of automation.

Mechanical Design

Understanding the skeleton of robots. I study Kinematics and Mechanism Design, using tools like SolidWorks/CAD to turn theoretical motion into physical reality.

Modeling & Control

The nervous system. I work with MATLAB & Simulink to model dynamic systems. My focus is on System Dynamics and classical control theories (PID) to ensure stability.

Project 01

Aurora Lens

A kinetic lighting prototype for exhibition spaces.

This project is a one-week team-based competition combining mechanism design and control theory on a real robotic platform.

The task required a 6-DOF robotic arm to grasp objects at arbitrary positions, place them onto a mobile vehicle, and trigger the vehicle to autonomously navigate a color-coded maze.

I was primarily responsible for the robotic arm control, focusing on motion planning, calibration, and reliable execution under real-world hardware constraints.

Role: Interaction + Visual Timeline: 8 weeks

Actuation rhythm, timing curves, and the final tuning notes.
